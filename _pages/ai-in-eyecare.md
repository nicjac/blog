### Most cases of blindness worldwide are preventable

- Around 250 million people in the world are blind or visually impaired [^1]
- 75% of those cases can be prevented or cured based on existing prophylaxis, drugs, or surgical interventions
- [Orbis](www.orbis.org), the NGO I work for, specializes in the logistics of prevention by training healthcare professionals in low- and middle-income countries, and ensure patients in these communities receive best-of-class eyecare and treatments.

### Urgent action is required

- The number of blind people expected to triple by 2050 [^2]
- It is likely that low- and middle-income countries will be disproportionately impacted.
